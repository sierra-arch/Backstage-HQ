# What We're Building (High Level)

Backstage OS is a calm, human-centered operating system for small business
owners. It helps founders:

- see their whole business in one place
- reduce judgment calls that lead to burnout
- offload repetitive thinking and admin
- feel proud of their business instead of overwhelmed by it

Backstage OS works without AI, and becomes more powerful when AI is
available — but never depends on it. **AI is an assistant, not an
authority.**

## Core Architecture (Very Important)

Backstage OS has three distinct layers:

1. **Backstage OS (The Spine)** — the internal infrastructure/operating
   system. Handles workflows, approvals, vaults, health snapshots, build
   logic. Immutable, safe, future-proof. Never marketed as a product or
   entitlement. Nothing breaks if AI is removed.
2. **Backstage (Default Skin)** — the default client-facing experience. Calm
   neutral palette with Backstage green. Backstage language, metaphors, tone.
   Users never start from a "blank slate."
3. **Custom Skins (Optional, Layered)** — created via onboarding + settings.
   Reflect the client's own branding, language, tone. Reversible and
   editable. Never allowed to override safety, clarity, or approval rules.

**Rule: Skins may affect presentation, never process.**

## Values Charter (Standing Constraints) — LOCKED

All decisions must pass these:

1. **Human agency before automation** — AI drafts, suggests, prepares —
   humans approve.
2. **Power must be bounded to be ethical** — no unrestricted actions, no
   silent automation.
3. **Safety is a design requirement** — risk slows the system down by
   default.
4. **Clarity is a form of care** — reduce cognitive and emotional load.
5. **Responsibility is shared, not centralized** — everything is traceable
   and reviewable.
6. **Access without exploitation** — expand capacity without removing
   autonomy.
7. **If trust and growth conflict, trust wins.**

Key questions to constantly ask before shipping anything:

- Does this increase agency or replace it?
- Who could this accidentally harm?
- If this fails, who pays the price?
- Does this concentrate power or distribute it?
- Would I feel okay if this were used imperfectly?

## Onboarding Philosophy — LOCKED

**Onboarding is a one-time, intentional ritual, not a setup checklist.**

- Runs once on first entry.
- Can only be re-taken via Settings.
- Uses founder-heart questions (from Week One workbook themes).
- Focuses on: purpose, who they serve, how they serve, boundaries and
  non-negotiables, vision and capacity.
- Each step reveals **who they are becoming**, not what they lack.

### Progressive Reveal (dopamine, but ethical)

Order of reveal:
1. Background adjusts first
2. Then language
3. Then tone
4. Then color
5. Then logo

Ends with a gentle celebration — **confetti is allowed**.

**Final moment:** "Your space is ready."

### Witness Statement (Onboarding Output) — LOCKED

At the end of onboarding, the system generates and stores a Witness
Statement. Not editable (only re-generated if onboarding is re-run).

Purpose: grounding, alignment, future reference.

Template:
> "This system was shaped around serving [who], in a way that honors
> [boundaries], with capacity for [vision]."

Stored in: Business Identity. Viewable from Dashboard (read-only).
Referenced contextually in Build Packs.

## Dashboard Principles

The dashboard should feel: calming, spacious, grounding, inspiring (not
performative).

### Dashboard Guardrail (Locked Rule)

**The dashboard never asks for urgency.**

Disallowed language: "You should," "Overdue," "Fix this now."
Allowed language: "Here's what's present," "Here's one next build if you
want it."

The dashboard performs **orientation, not evaluation**.

### Key elements

- **Welcome card** — orientation, not metrics.
- **Business Health snapshot** — icon-based, no judgment words, expandable
  for meaning.
- **Ask Backstage** — suggestions only; nothing happens without approval.
- **Next Build** — auto-suggested, one clear step at a time.
- **Business Vault** — Delivery / Brand / Systems / Marketing / Capacity.
- Optional "Work with Backstage" section ��� kept gentle, no pressure.

## Business Health Model

- Abstract icons only, at a glance.
- No red/yellow/green scoring.
- No "good/bad" language.
- Expandable panel explains meaning when the user asks.
- Purpose: awareness, not evaluation.

## Build Packs

Build Packs are the primary way work gets done. They are: draft-first,
approval-based, reusable. They auto-suggest based on onboarding answers.
**Nothing runs automatically without consent.**

Example packs: Client Deliverables Pack, Client Journey Pack, Capacity Pack,
Offer & Onboarding Pack.

### Build Pack Ethical Constraint (Locked Rule)

**A Pack can prepare work, but never complete work.**

Every Pack must end with:
1. Preview — what has been prepared
2. Explanation — why this matters / what it affects
3. Decision — approve, revise, or pause

No Pack (AI or non-AI) may execute, publish, or finalize without explicit
human approval.

## AI Philosophy

AI is optional and conditional. If AI is available, it may:
- help draft content using founder answers
- suggest color palettes
- generate abstract logo concepts only if no logo is uploaded

AI never:
- publishes without approval
- overrides accessibility or safety rules
- forces branding decisions
- replaces human judgment

Nothing breaks if AI is removed.

## V1 Acceptance Criteria (Stop Shipping Rule) — LOCKED

V1 is successful if — and only if — all five are true:

1. User logs in and feels calmer than before.
2. They can see their business without judgment.
3. They know exactly one next step.
4. Nothing happens without their consent.
5. They leave feeling capable, not behind.

If all five are met: stop shipping and pilot.

## Track A — MVP you can ship fast (reference/historical — Backstage HQ has
since gone further than this stack, kept for context)

Stack originally proposed: Softr or Glide (portal layer), Google Drive
(Vault).

### Track A — V1 Dashboard Scope (reference)

- **Tile 1: Business Vault** — five fixed folders (Delivery, Brand, Systems,
  Marketing, Capacity), each linking to a Google Drive folder containing
  template docs, draft Build Pack outputs, and a read-only Witness Statement
  copy.
- **Tile 2: Next Build** — one guided form. Inputs: business name, audience
  label, tone words, offer type, current priority. Output (copy + paste
  only): client welcome/onboarding message, next steps checklist,
  offboarding/review request. Rules: nothing auto-sends, nothing publishes,
  user chooses what to use.
- **Tile 3: Business Health** — static abstract icon snapshot, no
  scores/colors/judgment. On click: gentle explanation + one suggested "Next
  Build" (optional).

### Experience rules (Track A)

One primary action per screen. No metrics. No urgency language. Calm,
spacious layout.

## Track B — Backstage OS Spine (living spec)

Documentation first, code later. Protects long-term integrity while Track A
ships fast.

### Core data models (draft)

**BusinessIdentity:** purpose, who_they_serve, how_they_serve, boundaries,
vision, witness_statement.

**VisualSkin:** base_palette, tone_language, typography_tokens,
logo_reference (optional).

**VaultItem:** domain (Delivery / Brand / Systems / Marketing / Capacity),
type (doc, checklist, draft), source (Pack / manual).

**PackRun:** pack_type, inputs, draft_outputs, explanation, approval_status.

### Pack framework (Spine rule)

All Packs: draft-first, approval-based, reusable, consent-driven. No Pack
may: auto-execute, cross domains silently, bypass explanation or decision.

### Guardrails & approvals logic

Any external communication requires review. Any risk increases friction
(extra confirmation). All actions are traceable. No silent automation.

### Future integrations (deferred)

Storage: local → Supabase (done — Backstage HQ already runs on Supabase).
AI: optional assistant only. Team roles & permissions. Multi-user approval
flows.

### Design enforcement questions (always ask)

Same five questions as the Values Charter above.

### Final Rule

**If trust and growth conflict, trust wins.**

Backstage OS is calm by design, ethical by constraint, and durable by
choice.

## Notes on reconciling this with Backstage HQ's actual build (added by Claude)

Backstage HQ (the live app at backstage-dashboard.vercel.app) is further
along than the "Track A" MVP described above — it's a real Supabase-backed
multi-company dashboard, not the Softr/Glide/Drive-vault MVP. Track A's
literal tile structure is historical context, not a spec to retrofit. The
parts of this document that ARE actionable specs for the current build:

- The **Values Charter**, **Onboarding Philosophy**, **Dashboard Guardrail**,
  and **Build Pack Ethical Constraint** are locked rules and should govern
  every new feature going forward, including ones already in flight.
- "Next Build" (one auto-suggested clear step) maps naturally onto the
  existing Today's Focus concept, but should adopt the guardrail language
  (no urgency words) and, where it triggers system action, the
  preview/explain/decide pattern.
- The **Witness Statement** and **Progressive Reveal** onboarding pattern
  should replace a generic "setup wizard" — this is richer and more specific
  than a standard SaaS onboarding flow and should be built as described here.
